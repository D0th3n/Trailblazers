TRAILBLAZERS — HUMAN NOBLE FAMILIES
Timestamp: May 4, 2026 (America/New_York)

PURPOSE:
This file defines the major human noble houses, their revolt-era roots, public identities, government ties, and naming/class logic in Acerima.

=====================================
CORE CONCEPT
=====================================

- Human nobility in Acerima grew out of specialized survivor groups from the Dragon Revolt.
- Over centuries these groups became noble houses, government branches, military traditions, and elite family networks.
- Unlike the Elven Kingdom's clan structure, Aceriman nobility is closer to an English/U.S. hybrid:
  - old surnames
  - formal registry
  - public service dynasties
  - military/service houses
  - wealthy houses
  - new-money noble registration

=====================================
SURNAME AND CLASS LOGIC
=====================================

- A surname shows ancestral tradition.
- A family line shows political status.
- The House Registry determines legal nobility.

Commoners:
- usually do not carry formal house surnames
- often identify as given name + of + birthplace

Nobles:
- carry a formal house surname

=====================================
FAMILY LINE STRUCTURE
=====================================

Human noble families have two broad line categories:
1. main bloodline family
2. lesser family lines

- Lesser lines are still noble, but main bloodline descendants carry higher prestige and inheritance weight.

=====================================
NEW NOBILITY
=====================================

- Commoners do not casually become part of old noble houses.
- Entry into an existing house normally requires:
  - marriage
  - birth
- A commoner can found a new noble family through the registry, but it requires:
  - proof of funds
  - land/estate ownership
  - stable income
  - family records
  - kingdom approval
  - heavy paperwork

=====================================
ADOPTION
=====================================

- Adoption is not a major modernized legal institution.
- Orphan care is usually handled through:
  - churches
  - priests
  - local families
  - village records
- Raising an orphan does not automatically make them a legal noble heir.
