TRAILBLAZERS — HIGHGUARD HIERARCHY AND DUTIES
Timestamp: April 22, 2026 (America/New_York)

PURPOSE:
This file defines the kingdom combat/service hierarchy and why Embrum and Oren are involved in the Anozira investigation.

COMBAT/SERVICE LADDER:
1. Regular knights
2. Mercenaries
3. Swordmasters
4. Highguard members

PUBLIC LIMIT:
- People from the regular public can rise up to swordmaster.
- Becoming Highguard requires royal selection.
- A candidate must be chosen or nominated by the king or royal family to become Highguard.

HIGHGUARD STATUS:
- Highguard members are among the most capable people in the kingdom.
- They are treated as superstrong anomalies and public celebrities.
- They are expected to protect the kingdom and wider world from major threats.

EXTRA TASKS:
- Because Highguard members are so capable, the kingdom often assigns them difficult extra problems.
- These assignments are often handled during a Highguard member's free time when they are not dealing with major threats.
- The tasks can include practical, local, or embarrassing problems the kingdom does not want to handle directly.
- This also keeps Highguard members visible around the kingdom, reinforcing public trust and celebrity.

ANOZIRA ASSIGNMENT:
- Oren and Embrum learned about Anozira because the Chief of the Highguard assigned the matter to Embrum.
- The kingdom treated Anozira as a difficult local problem that could be pushed onto a Highguard member.
- Embrum brought Oren because Embrum is under orders to keep Oren under watch after Dravrah.
- Oren is not on the Anozira mission as an independent hero; he is there because Embrum cannot leave him unsupervised.

EMBRUM'S ROLE:
- Embrum is a Highguard member and Oren's assigned supervisor.
- Embrum sees the Anozira task as both an investigation and a controlled field test for Oren.
- Embrum remains duty-bound: if Oren loses control and threatens civilians, Embrum will stop him.
