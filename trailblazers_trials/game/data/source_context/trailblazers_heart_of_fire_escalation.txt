TRAILBLAZERS — HEART OF FIRE ESCALATION

PURPOSE:
This file defines the mystery chain for the Anozira / Heart of Fire arc after the first Moglim encounter.

CORE CHAIN:
- Moglim fight = symptom
- Ruzen mines = practical heat-source clue site tied to underground reservoirs
- Pyroclast Titan = visible central threat, charging deep in Ruzen and superheating the mountain
- Embracron = deeper later reveal inside the Ruzen crisis
- Severence = hidden hand

IMPORTANT:
- Early scenes must not reveal Severence by name.
- Early scenes must not reveal Crons or the Embracron.
- Characters should discover the crisis through practical evidence.
- The audience should slowly notice this is not a random village monster problem.

DISCOVERY SEQUENCE:
1. Oren and Embrum defeat or drive off Moglim in the nairn fields.
2. They return to Anozira with scorched Moglim remains, ash-covered srag, and evidence the Moglim were feeding from damaged land.
3. Embrum explains the wells and ground heat matter more than the recycler: something is heating the underground reservoirs until they steam away.
4. The mayor mentions the Ruzen mines as the strongest practical lead.
5. The Ruzen mines are treated as the likely deep heat source, not as a boss dungeon.
6. The mine investigation reveals heat bursts, steam pressure, glowing Ruzen seams, and tools too hot to hold.
7. Deeper clues suggest the mine is not naturally destabilizing: geometric cuts, unfamiliar runic scoring, precise crystal fractures, missing high-density Ruzen clusters, and Moglim nesting around one chamber.
8. The mine behaves like a charging furnace: heat pulses, steam hisses, glowing dust, and Oren/Jotunn sensing something consuming power before Embrum understands it.
9. The mine is revealed to be above/near the charging source, not the true source itself.
10. A lower chamber reveals an enormous melted passage, an impossible footprint or drag mark, and old Ruzen veins ripped clean out of the stone.
11. A pulse event follows: the mine shudders like something colossal took a step.
12. The surface crisis worsens: wells steam, more srag turns to ash, nairn collapses, and heat haze rises over the mine ridge.
13. First Titan reveal should happen at dusk beyond the fields or mine ridge. A "hill" moves, glowing cracks appear, stone breaks loose, and Embrum realizes it is not a mountain.
14. Later reveal: the Titan was deployed by Severence to contain and capture a forming Embracron, not merely to destroy Anozira.

RUZEN MINES:
- Ruzen is Anozira's second-most valuable export after nairn.
- The mines are currently shut down.
- Shutdown cause is recent extreme heat and pressure, not one simple cave-in.
- Reported symptoms: sudden heat bursts, glowing tunnel cracks, overheated tools, steam, and ground heat climbing toward the village wells.
- At first, Embrum should believe a deep heat source around Ruzen is heating the underground reservoirs connected to Anozira's wells.

HIDDEN SEVERENCE HINTS:
- Artificial channels cut into Ruzen veins.
- Foreign residue such as blackened wax, bone ash, or hardened resin seal.
- Select high-density Ruzen clusters removed while lower-value ore remains.
- One recurring mark where channels begin, not yet identifiable to Oren or Embrum.
- These hints should imply "someone did something down here" without naming Severence.

EMBRUM'S END-OF-MINE CONCLUSION:
- The crisis is bigger than a village infestation.
- The underground reservoirs are being cooked by a concentrated deep heat source.
- The mine was tampered with.
- He should remain serious and observant without jumping to the hidden truth.

OREN'S END-OF-MINE CONCLUSION:
- The heat feels familiar.
- The stone feels charged.
- The heat feels like something is feeding.
- Jotunn notices shaped heat before Oren trusts himself.
- Oren should not speak with full confidence because he still believes the voice may be madness.

JOTUNN EARLY HINT LINES:
- "This heat is shaped."
- "Something is drinking the mountain."
- "This is not how earth burns on its own."

ARC END STATE:
- The village may be saved or partially stabilized.
- The Titan may be destroyed, driven off, or revealed as part of a larger Severence containment operation.
- Later story can reveal the Embracron: a fire Cron formed from an unrecovered miner's body in the fire-weather convergence zone deep in Ruzen.
- Embrum should keep one suspicious object, carving, or residue sample from the mine.
- He should not fully explain it to Oren yet.
