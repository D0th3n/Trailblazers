TRAILBLAZERS — QANA HUNTING SYSTEM
Timestamp: April 22, 2026 (America/New_York)

PURPOSE:
This file defines Qana hunting as the structured human interaction with Qana creatures for combat, capture, harvesting, research, and environmental control.

=====================================
CORE CONCEPT
=====================================

Qana hunting is not just combat.

It includes:
- combat
- capture
- material harvesting
- field documentation
- environmental control
- guild verification
- official knowledge updates

Hunting is a profession, a survival practice, an economic system, and a knowledge pipeline.

=====================================
COMBAT STRUCTURE
=====================================

Combat is hybrid and role-dependent.

1. QANA CASTERS / WIZARDS

- strong spell output
- weak physical combat
- rely on distance, control, and elemental pressure
- often poor sword fighters

2. SWORDSMEN / NON-QANA TRAINED FIGHTERS

- strong physical combat
- limited Qana education
- may only know basic spells such as tiny ember creation or push pulse
- often cannot maintain advanced spell structures

3. KINGDOM KNIGHTS

- trained in both swordsmanship and Qana
- mandatory sword school
- mandatory Qana school
- highly disciplined
- limited by kingdom orders and jurisdiction
- cannot take every job freely

4. MERCENARIES

- mixed skill sets
- flexible job acceptance
- may be physical specialists, Qana specialists, scouts, harvesters, or mixed fighters
- can take jobs for payment if their guild master allows it
- often operate in teams because no single mercenary type covers every need

=====================================
TEAM DYNAMIC
=====================================

Most serious hunts require multiple roles and coordinated strategy.

Example:
- swordsman distracts or anchors the creature
- Qana caster attacks the elemental weakness
- knight controls the flow if kingdom authority is involved
- support mercenaries scout, trap, harvest, or evacuate civilians

This makes Qana hunting a team profession rather than a single universal hero job.

=====================================
FUTURE GAMEPLAY LOOP
=====================================

Use this loop for future Qana hunting games, school missions, guild jobs, and creature-focused RPG systems.

1. BRIEFING

A teacher, guild representative, knight officer, CAC contact, or local authority explains the mission.

The briefing may provide:
- creature type
- known habitat
- known behavior
- known weaknesses
- expected threat tier
- capture, kill, harvest, escort, or investigation objective

Important:
Briefing information may be incomplete, outdated, biased, or wrong.
Known weaknesses can be wrong if the creature adapted, if records are old, or if another region's Book entry is being trusted too much.

2. PREPARATION

The player chooses how to approach the mission.

Preparation can include:
- spells
- weapons
- tools
- traps
- team members
- combat role
- support role
- harvest tools
- capture equipment
- environmental protection

Preparation should matter because no single role can handle every hunt perfectly.

3. ENCOUNTER

The creature's real behavior is revealed in the field.

The encounter may not match expectations.
The creature may:
- behave differently than the Book entry suggested
- use an unexpected raw Qana effect
- have a different temperament than expected
- be protecting territory, young, food, or a Qana source
- be stronger or weaker because of the biome
- expose a new adaptation that needs documentation

4. LIVE ADAPTATION

This is the most important gameplay phase.

The player must react to:
- environment
- creature behavior
- team weaknesses
- elemental mismatch
- failed preparation
- unreliable briefing information
- changing battlefield conditions

This is player adaptation, not creature evolution.
Creatures do not evolve mid-fight, but players must adjust their strategy in real time.

5. OUTCOME

Possible outcomes include:
- kill
- capture
- retreat
- drive away
- stabilize the environment
- harvest materials
- fail the mission but survive
- discover new information and withdraw

6. REPORT

The mission report contributes to:
- Book of Qana Creatures updates
- guild reputation
- team reputation
- school grades or field certification
- CAC research
- kingdom records
- regional creature knowledge
- future briefing accuracy

Reports close the hunt loop.
The player's field experience can become future world knowledge.

=====================================
WEAKNESS SYSTEM
=====================================

All Qana creatures have elemental weaknesses.

Core rule:
- creatures resist their own element
- creatures are vulnerable to opposing elements

Important:
- weaknesses are stable
- creatures cannot change weaknesses instantly
- creatures cannot turn weaknesses off at will
- adaptation takes weeks to months
- forced adaptation requires harsh and sustained environmental pressure

Because Qana creatures adapt to environments over time, their elemental profile can change, but not in a single battle.

=====================================
ADAPTATION RULE
=====================================

Creatures do not evolve.
They adapt.

Time range:
- fastest known adaptation window: about 28 days
- slowest common adaptation window: about 6 months

Triggers:
- environmental pressure
- repeated exposure
- survival stress
- severe biome conditions
- sustained elemental pressure

Results:
- resistance changes
- behavior shifts
- physical changes become possible
- aggression level may shift
- habitat preference may shift

Adaptation is not instant and should never be written as mid-fight evolution.

=====================================
HUNT OUTCOMES
=====================================

All outcomes are valid:

- kill
- capture
- harvest
- drive away
- relocate
- document and withdraw
- stabilize the environment instead of fighting

Killing is common, but not the only outcome.
Capture is rare and difficult.
Harvesting almost always happens if a body remains.

Rule:
Almost no body goes unused.

Creature materials are valuable to:
- poor communities
- guilds
- CAC researchers
- smiths
- healers
- Qana technicians
- spell-tool makers
- food suppliers when safe

=====================================
KNOWLEDGE SYSTEM
=====================================

The Book of Qana Creatures is shared knowledge across the kingdom.
It is used by multiple sectors of society, including schools, guilds, knights, CAC researchers, and local authorities.

HOW IT UPDATES:

1. Discovery
- hunter, mercenary, knight, student, traveler, or civilian encounters new information
- this can involve a new creature or new behavior from a known creature

2. Report to guild
- the witness reports to the nearest guild desk
- informal rumor is not enough for official records

3. Guild verification mission
- the guild sends mercenaries or approved field teams to confirm the information
- verification may involve observation, tracks, combat, capture, harvested parts, or repeated sightings

4. Confirmed documentation
- verified findings are written into an official guild report

5. Submission to kingdom information department
- the guild sends the official report to the kingdom's information department

6. Official record update
- the kingdom reviews the report and updates the Book of Qana Creatures if accepted

Rule:
The kingdom only accepts verified information from official sources.
Unverified information is ignored or treated as rumor.

=====================================
INFORMATION GAP
=====================================

Not all nations share creature knowledge.

Result:
- some regions know more than others
- the same creature may have different threat records across borders
- a creature can be common knowledge in one nation and an unknown threat in another
- guilds, schools, and kingdoms may keep valuable records private

Knowledge is power.

=====================================
CREATURE ABILITIES
=====================================

Qana creatures can naturally perform spell-like effects.

Mechanism:
- emotional impulse
- instinctual intent
- direct Qana-field manipulation

This resembles Beastmen casting style.

Important:
- not structured like human spells
- not usually based on formal circles, chants, or trained spell geometry
- raw and instinctive
- often unpredictable
- shaped by desire, fear, aggression, hunger, territory, or survival instinct

=====================================
LIMITATIONS
=====================================

Qana creatures cannot:
- evolve mid-fight
- instantly change elemental nature
- instantly erase weaknesses
- bypass adaptation time

They can use Qana instinctively, but they cannot rewrite their biology in a single encounter.

=====================================
WORLD IMPACT
=====================================

1. Qana hunting is a real profession.
2. Guilds are essential because they organize jobs, teams, reports, and verification.
3. CAC depends on hunters for materials and data.
4. Poor communities rely on creature parts and rarely leave bodies unharvested.
5. The Book of Qana Creatures makes verified knowledge a strategic resource.
6. Mixed parties matter because no single training path covers every hunt.
7. Kingdom knights are versatile but politically limited; mercenaries are flexible but less standardized.
