TRAILBLAZERS ECOLOGY SYSTEM FILE
Timestamp: April 21, 2026 (America/New_York)

PURPOSE:
This file defines the ecological naming, logic, and rules of the Trailblazers world.
All references to generic Earth terms (e.g., "grass") must be replaced with in-universe terminology.

=====================================
CORE ECOLOGY RULE
=====================================

All plant life is influenced by:
1. Climate (base form)
2. Mana Density (enhancement/amplification)

Plants are the foundation of all ecosystems.
NO EARTH PLANT NAMES SHOULD BE USED.

=====================================
FOUNDATIONAL PLANT TYPES (MANDATORY TERMS)
=====================================

SRAG (s-rag)
- Ground cover plant
- Equivalent to grass
- Covers most plains and open land
- Function:
  - Stabilizes terrain
  - Base food source for herbivores
  - Absorbs ambient mana
- Visual:
  - Slight glow in high mana zones
  - Can ripple subtly with mana flow

NAIRN (n-air-n)
- Grain-based crop
- Combination of wheat + grain
- Used for:
  - Food production
  - Animal feed
- Grown in farm regions

SINU (si-nu)
- Leafy crop
- Replaces spinach/kale type foods
- Stable, low magic influence
- Used in daily meals

=====================================
ECOLOGICAL LANGUAGE RULE
=====================================

INVALID TERMS:
- grass
- wheat
- plants
- crops (generic usage)

VALID TERMS:
- srag
- nairn
- sinu
- flora (only when generalizing)

EXAMPLE CONVERSION:

WRONG:
"The slime bursts across the grass."

CORRECT:
"The slime bursts across the srag."

=====================================
MAGICAL ECOLOGY INTERACTION
=====================================

Plants react to mana density in real-time.

LOW MANA:
- Normal growth
- Stable ecosystems

MEDIUM MANA:
- Enhanced growth
- Slight glow or visual distortion

HIGH MANA:
- Rapid overgrowth
- Subspecies adaptation
- Environmental instability

=====================================
BIOME-SPECIFIC PLANT BEHAVIOR
=====================================

PLAINS (SRAG FIELDS):
- Dominated by srag
- Stable terrain
- Low threat

FORESTS:
- Large tree systems
- Dense root networks
- High mana absorption

DESERTS:
- Sparse flora
- Heat-reactive plant structures
- Mana + heat = ignition risk

FROST REGIONS:
- Slow growth flora
- Mana stored as crystal-like structures

JUNGLES:
- Extreme plant density
- Rapid growth cycles
- High mana saturation

MOUNTAINS:
- Sparse but resilient flora
- Adapted to terrain fragmentation

=====================================
CREATURE INTERACTION WITH ECOLOGY
=====================================

Herbivores:
- Consume srag and processed nairn

Predators:
- Use terrain (srag, roots, elevation) for ambush

Low-tier creatures:
- Minimal ecological awareness

High-tier creatures:
- Use environment strategically

=====================================
IMPORTANT IMPLEMENTATION RULES (FOR CODEX)
=====================================

1. NEVER use Earth terminology for plant life.
2. Always replace:
   grass → srag
   wheat/grain → nairn
   leafy greens → sinu

3. Environmental descriptions must reflect mana influence:
   Example:
   "wet splash of unstable mana across the srag"

4. Ecology should reinforce immersion:
   - Naming consistency is REQUIRED
   - No fallback to generic language
