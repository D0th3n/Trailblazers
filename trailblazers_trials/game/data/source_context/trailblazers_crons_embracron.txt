TRAILBLAZERS — CRONS AND EMBRACRON CONTEXT

PURPOSE:
This file defines Crons as a later-story Qana biology concept and locks the Ruzen mines Embracron reveal.

CRONS:
- Crons are ancient elemental creatures formed when extreme Qana density and elemental environmental conditions converge around an exposed dead body.
- Crons are born only from exposed dead bodies in areas of high Qana density and dominant elemental influence.
- All Crons are corpse-born with no exceptions.
- Crons are not like dragons.
- Dragons are conscious biological beings stabilized around Pure Qana.
- Crons are elemental force operating through corpse-shaped vessels.
- Crons are not truly intelligent or fully conscious.
- Cron behavior follows the dominant element that created them.

FORMATION:
- A Cron can form when a body is left unburied in a highly unstable elemental zone.
- The surrounding Qana and dominant element overtake the corpse.
- The body decomposes into the dominant element and reanimates as a Cron.
- After roughly one hour of exposure and conversion, the original soul is gone.
- Once that threshold passes, the body can no longer be reclaimed as the person it once was.
- This is why burial, corpse recovery, and recovery of fallen workers/soldiers are taken seriously across the world.

LEGENDARY CRONS:
- Legendary Crons are not formed by a different rule.
- They are legendary because the corpse they form from was powerful.
- A Cron born from the husk of a dragon is vastly more dangerous than a Cron born from a human, animal, or soldier.

NORMAL BEHAVIOR:
- Most Crons are extremely unintelligent.
- Most act almost entirely on instinct.
- Most do not strategize, reason, or speak.
- Most do not attack unless first provoked.
- Normal Crons usually stand dormant, drift toward concentrated Qana, or react only when disturbed.

KNOWN CRON TYPES:
- Lunacron
- Solacron
- Embracron
- Aquacron
- Electricron
- Floracron

LUNACRONS:
- Lunacrons are the primary exception to normal Cron behavior.
- Lunacrons are hostile on sight.
- Lunacrons consume the souls of living creatures.
- Lunacrons are often born when battlefields are left uncleansed, with lingering war Qana in the air and dead bodies exposed beneath lunar energy.
- In severe cases, entire Lunacron fields can form overnight and wipe out nearby settlements.
- This is why battlefield recovery, burial rites, church oversight, and graveyard crews are treated as serious civic and religious obligations.

ANOZIRA / RUZEN MINES EMBRACRON:
- An Embracron formed in the Ruzen mines.
- It began when a miner died deep within the mines and his body was never recovered.
- The inner Ruzen cave system had already become a fire-weather convergence zone.
- Underground fire tornadoes and unstable fire-element Qana remade the unrecovered body into a fire Cron.
- The Embracron is a fire Cron, not a dragon and not a conscious person returned from death.

SEVERENCE ROLE:
- Severence deployed a fire Titan-class golem into the Ruzen mines.
- Their goal was to contain and capture the forming Embracron.
- Their operation destabilized the region.
- The Titan charged itself by feeding on Ruzen fire energy and lava heat deep within the mountain.
- While charging, the Titan superheated the mountain and the underground water reservoirs connected to Anozira's wells.
- The drought was caused by reservoirs evaporating into steam and soil becoming too hot for young srag, nairn, and sinu.
- The destabilization contributed directly to the drought, Moglim spread, and escalating heat around Anozira.

REVEAL CONTROL:
- Do not reveal Crons, Embracron, or Severence's true goal in the earliest Anozira scenes.
- Early playable scenes should frame the crisis as drought, Moglim, Ruzen mine instability, and later the Titan-class threat.
- The Embracron reveal should arrive after enough evidence exists that the Ruzen mines are not merely overloaded and the Titan is not merely a wild monster.
- The emotional shape of the reveal should be: the village crisis was collateral damage from someone trying to capture something born from an unrecovered dead miner.
