TRAILBLAZERS — ELVEN POLITICS AND CULTURE
Timestamp: May 4, 2026 (America/New_York)

PURPOSE:
This file defines the political structure, gendered social roles, religion, offices, death culture, and broad house relationships of the Elven Kingdom.

IMPORTANT CANON NOTE:
- Use this file for elf government, religion, social roles, and institutions.
- For the current ruling royal house and named noble families, defer to `trailblazers_elf_noble_families.txt` when there is a conflict.

=====================================
CORE ELF IDENTITY
=====================================

- The Elven Kingdom is an Edo/Japanese-inspired fantasy society with sacred Latin spell language and Qana-centered religion.
- Major identity traits:
  - female Shogun rule
  - royal family tied to the current Shogun
  - elemental noble houses
  - female frontline warriors
  - male shamans
  - brush-wand air calligraphy
  - Mother Qana worship
  - strict family hierarchy
  - house loyalty
  - death oversight by Dark Elves
  - Wind Elves as a rare nomadic exception

=====================================
NAMING CULTURE
=====================================

- Elven names follow house/family name first, personal name second.
- Formal address prioritizes:
  - house first
  - rank second
  - personal name last or omitted in public formality

=====================================
SHOGUNATE STRUCTURE
=====================================

- The Elven Kingdom is ruled by a Shogun.
- The Shogun is always female.
- Her husband is always male.
- The current royal family is the family of the current Shogun rather than one permanently fixed dynasty.

=====================================
MAJOR HOUSES AND TERRITORIES
=====================================

Major elemental identities:
- High Elf / Electric
- Dark Elf
- Fire Elf
- Water / Ice Elf
- Flora Elf
- Wind Elf as a nomadic exception

Broad territory notes:
- High Elves: heartland / royal authority
- Dark Elves: death zones / Moon-Seal roles
- Fire Elves: desert-edge pressure zones
- Water/Ice Elves: mountain and coast
- Flora Elves: living forest regions
- Wind Elves: no fixed territory

=====================================
THREE GREAT OFFICES
=====================================

The Shogun directly oversees:

1. Blade Court
- female warriors
- duels
- military enforcement
- defense of the Shogun

2. Brush Court
- male shamans
- Latin spell poetry
- Mother Qana rites
- sacred archives
- poem safety laws

3. Moon-Seal Office
- death handling
- soul inspection
- corpse control
- Lunacron prevention
- death-Qana containment

=====================================
BLADE COURT
=====================================

- Governs the female warrior class.
- Female elves are the primary frontline combatants.
- They rely on:
  - sword skill
  - bows
  - armor
  - prepared Qana items
  - disciplined body movement
- They are not usually the primary raw poem casters.

=====================================
BRUSH COURT
=====================================

- Governs male shamans and sacred spell language.
- Male shamans use brush-shaped wands to write glowing Latin calligraphy in the air.
- Spell scaling:
  - 1 word = weak effect
  - 2 words = quick usable spell
  - phrase = stable combat spell
  - full poem = powerful precise spell
  - interrupted poem = partial release

=====================================
MOON-SEAL OFFICE
=====================================

- Led by Dark Elves.
- Answers directly to the Shogun.
- Handles:
  - corpses
  - death-heavy Qana
  - soul checks
  - battlefield recovery
  - Lunacron prevention
  - death-site quarantine
  - funeral safety

Dark Elves are feared by some but accepted as necessary.

=====================================
DARK ELF SOUL MAGIC
=====================================

- Dark Elves learned soul techniques through Death Dragon teachings.
- Their magic targets the soul rather than the physical body.

Example:
- Soul Slash
  - does not cut flesh
  - severs the soul from the body
  - kills instantly
  - sends the soul onward

Rule:
- Private Dark Elf soul magic outside Moon-Seal authority is illegal and treated as murder or forbidden deathcraft.

=====================================
MALE ELF / SHAMAN SYSTEM
=====================================

- Male elves are born with social expectation to become shamans.
- A shaman is a virgin male still under contract with Mother Qana through the River of Mother Qana blessing.
- The contract:
  - increases spiritual power
  - strengthens head-pool connection
  - weakens bodily rejuvenation and physical strength
- When the contract breaks through sexual union/reproduction:
  - raw Qana power decreases
  - bodily rejuvenation and strength return
  - martial ability becomes more viable

Two broad male paths:
- Shaman: high Qana, weaker body
- Former shaman / ordinary adult male: lower Qana, stronger body

=====================================
FEMALE ELF WARRIOR SYSTEM
=====================================

- Female elves are expected to defend family, house, shrine, and nation.
- They are trained in:
  - sword combat
  - bow combat
  - flash-step movement
  - armor use
  - duel etiquette
  - protecting shamans

=====================================
WANDERING BLADES
=====================================

- Wandering Blades are the elf equivalent of ronin-like free female warriors.
- They do not belong to guilds and are judged by reputation.

Reputation ladder:
- Unmarked Blade
- Road Blade
- House-Tested Blade
- Shrine-Tested Blade
- Moon-Tested Blade
- Banner-Worthy Blade

=====================================
WIND ELVES
=====================================

- Wind Elves are rare, global, pacifist, and legendary.
- They can fly and remain airborne for long periods.
- They are rarely seen inside the Elven Kingdom.
- They usually fight only in self-defense.
- They are associated with the Time Dragon.

=====================================
MOTHER QANA RELIGION
=====================================

- Modern elves worship Mother Qana.
- This religion descended from the Children of Mother Qana, who rejected dragon-mediated magic.
- Modern elf belief treats Mother Qana as sacred and spiritually central even though Qana itself is not literally conscious.
- Every Mother Qana spell is culturally also a rejection of dragon authority.

=====================================
BLACK RIVER CRISIS
=====================================

- Mother Qana fanatics once dumped bodies into the River of Mother Qana, believing it would cleanse them.
- Instead, the river weakened and death-Qana corruption worsened.
- Consequences included:
  - weaker shamans
  - unstable blessings
  - nightly Lunacron formation
  - village panic
- Dark Elves were brought back into authority to fix the disaster.
- This crisis elevated Dark Elf legal status and helped create the Moon-Seal Office.

=====================================
ELVEN DEATH HANDLING
=====================================

Modern process:
1. Family reports death
2. Moon-Seal / Dark Elf handlers inspect the body
3. Soul condition and death-Qana risk are checked
4. Body is sealed and prepared
5. Family chooses rite if safe
6. Site is monitored if needed

Typical house preferences:
- High Elves: ceremonial burial
- Fire Elves: cremation
- Water/Ice Elves: sealed coastal or mountain rites
- Flora Elves: forest return under Moon-Seal oversight
- Dark Elves: dangerous-body direct handling
