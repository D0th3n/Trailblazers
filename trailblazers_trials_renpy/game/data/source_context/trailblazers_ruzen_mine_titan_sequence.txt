TRAILBLAZERS — RUZEN MINE TITAN SEQUENCE
Timestamp: April 22, 2026 (America/New_York)

PURPOSE:
This file defines the next Heart of Fire script sequence after Oren and Embrum reach the Ruzen mine entrance.

MINER / FOREMAN TESTIMONY:
- Ruzen mines have always been dangerous because of fire-Qana weather phenomena.
- Miners are used to flame bursts, fire tornadoes, and watching Qana build before it becomes unsafe.
- The danger changed two to three months ago.
- The mine temperature began rising after a deep expedition to harvest larger Ruzen crystals before the next Eldran shipment.
- The foreman pushed the expedition because he wanted to meet quota and make extra money.
- During the expedition, Qana built too fast and caused flame tornadoes.
- The miners were trapped briefly and escaped, but two men died in the flames.
- One dead miner had a father waiting for him.
- One dead miner had a mother and little sister waiting for him.
- Their bodies were lost inside the mines.

VILLAGE CONSEQUENCE:
- After the deaths, villagers began seeing the crisis as punishment.
- The wells began producing hot or boiling water directly from the ground.
- Then the water burned away and left wells empty.
- Emergency water-barrel shipments had to be requested from a nearby city.
- The first shipment arrived roughly three months after the request.
- No one knows when the next shipment will come.

DESCENT:
- Oren and Embrum enter the mine to find the cause.
- Deeper inside, they encounter more Moglim.
- A herd/group of Moglim is called a Mogul.
- Example usage: "Careful. I saw Moglim nearby. I think it was a whole Mogul."
- Fire-Qana weather becomes more volatile deeper in the mine.
- Oren and Embrum are high-level fire-Qana users, so the heat is dangerous but also strangely refreshing.
- Humans can refill Qana pools from dense atmospheric Qana.
- Miners cannot breathe in the heat, while Oren and Embrum can function and even recover from it.
- High-level Qana users can feel or almost smell dense Qana.
- Oren and Embrum follow the strongest Qana density down the proper cave path.

TITAN REVEAL:
- Oren and Embrum discover a gigantic fire Titan golem.
- Embrum identifies it as a tier 3 golem.
- Tier 3 golems are weapons of war, used to knock down kingdom walls and enable invasions.
- Summoning a Titan golem is difficult and illegal unless demanded by the kingdom for battle.
- Embrum does not know Severence or the Embracron are involved.
- Embrum concludes illegal mages/wizards may be interfering with the kingdom.

PLAN:
- Oren warns that fighting a Titan underground could collapse the mountain, destroy Ruzen resources, and trap them.
- They plan to keep the Titan's movements contained while one person charges a decisive strike at its Qana core.
- A Qana core is the crystal heart/battery that powers a golem.
- Embrum is more skilled and takes the distraction role.
- Oren charges the attack while listening to the voice inside him.
- Oren's spear shot combines Pure Qana pressure from Jötunn with Oren's own fire-Qana output.
- Oren destroys the Titan's Qana core with a twin spear strike.

RESULT:
- The Titan was causing the drought by eating lava/heat inside the mountain and emitting powerful pulses of fire-Qana.
- Those pulses heated the ground and evaporated the underground reservoirs connected to Anozira's wells.
- Destroying the Titan stops the pulse source.
- The reservoirs should refill naturally over time with kingdom water shipments helping the village survive the recovery period.

REVEAL CONTROL:
- Do not reveal Severence or the Cron/Embracron truth in this sequence.
- Oren and Embrum should leave believing the problem was an illegal war golem left under the mountain by malicious mages or wizards.

END OF FILE
