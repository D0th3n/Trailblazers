TRAILBLAZERS — ACERIMA POLITICS AND CULTURE
Timestamp: May 4, 2026 (America/New_York)

PURPOSE:
This file defines the current political structure, social identity, naming logic, military framework, religious life, and provincial culture of the Human Kingdom of Acerima.

=====================================
CORE HUMAN IDENTITY
=====================================

- Acerima formed after the Dragon Age Revolt from scattered freed peoples rather than one preexisting unified state.
- Modern humans believe they survived by organizing chaos into law.
- Human public culture is shaped by:
  - liberation from dragons
  - devotion to the Highest Order
  - reverence for the royal line of Jyra Acerima
  - old Chainless tribal roots
  - military academies and knights
  - provincial identity
  - burial law
  - fear of uncontrolled Qana
  - pride in human institutions

=====================================
NAMING CULTURE
=====================================

- Modern Aceriman names descend from old Chainless naming systems.
- Commoner naming often follows:
  - given name + of + birth location
- Noble naming uses formal house surnames.
- Chainless people preserve the most sacred and culturally loaded forms of human naming.

=====================================
KINGDOM GOVERNMENT
=====================================

- Acerima is ruled by the royal family descended from Jyra and Arthur.
- Publicly, Jyra is remembered as the first prophet blessed by the Highest Order.
- The public does not know Jyra is the daughter of the Highest Order.
- The current queen is Jyana.
- Acerima is not ruled by the monarch alone. The kingdom is divided into sectors and administrative branches.

Major sectors:
- Crown Seat / Royal Family
- Queen Jyana's Council
- Aceriman War Table
- Information Sector
- Religious priesthood and gravewarden network
- Crown Artifice Council (CAC)
- Provincial governors
- Highguard

=====================================
QUEEN AND COUNCIL
=====================================

- Queen Jyana rules from the capital.
- Her council filters most political and administrative matters before they reach her.
- The most sensitive matters may bypass the council entirely.

Council-level matters:
- provincial disputes
- border concerns
- noble complaints
- military budget issues
- CAC reports
- diplomacy

Direct-to-queen matters:
- threats to the royal bloodline
- classified Highguard missions
- dragon-related danger
- major Lunacron outbreaks
- Severence activity
- Divine Qana threats

=====================================
PROVINCES
=====================================

- Acerima is divided into provinces that function somewhat like states.
- Each province has:
  - 1 main city
  - 2 villages
  - 2 forts
- Each province is overseen by a noble governor who also holds a council seat.

Example province:
- Northreach
  - main city: Ainigriv
  - villages: Cask and Fen
  - governor: Tessouat / Governor Tesso
  - roles: northern defense, diplomacy, military academy presence, teleportation tower protection

=====================================
MILITARY FRAMEWORK
=====================================

- Each province maintains roughly 40,000 active military personnel.
- Forts function as large mixed-branch military settlements.
- Acerima is not commanded by one supreme general.
- It is governed militarily by the Aceriman War Table.

War Table seats:
- Marshal of the Crown Army: Aponi Tall-Elk
- Admiral of the Tide Fleet: Takoda Blue-Reed
- Archmarshal of the Sky Corps: Halona Storm-Eye
- Warden-General of the Province Guard: Makya Stone-River
- Highguard Guildmaster: Quanah
- War Table Secretary: Winona Red-Quill

Major branches:
- Crown Army
- Tide Fleet
- Sky Corps
- Province Guard
- Highguard

=====================================
KNIGHT ACCESS AND PROGRESSION
=====================================

- Knighthood is not legally restricted to nobles.
- In practice, nobles have overwhelming advantages in training, tutoring, equipment, and academy access.
- Commoners can become knights, but the system is materially unequal.

Knight progression:
1. Cadet
2. Field Apprentice
3. Prospect Knight
4. Knight
5. Senior Knight
6. Squad Leader
7. Squadron Leader
8. Captain
9. Commander
10. Branch Marshal / Admiral / Archmarshal / Warden-General

=====================================
PRIVATE GUARDS
=====================================

- Private guards are common among nobles and wealthy humans.
- They require licensing for armed public operation in sensitive areas.
- They are loyal to the family paying them, not the kingdom.
- They cannot legally obstruct an active kingdom investigation.

=====================================
CHAINLESS TRIBES
=====================================

- The Chainless Tribes are Aceriman citizens who refused full modernization.
- They preserve:
  - old names
  - oral history
  - old spiritual practices
  - border survival rites
  - anti-centralized-government instincts
- They are not foreign to Acerima; they are one of its oldest living roots.

=====================================
RELIGION
=====================================

- Humans worship the Highest Order.
- Public belief centers on:
  - Jyra as a blessed prophet
  - the royal line as sacred
  - the sun as a sign of closeness to the Highest Order
  - burial under strong sunlight as spiritually prestigious
- The hidden truth is deeper:
  - Jyra is the daughter of the Highest Order
  - the royal bloodline is directly divine
  - Arthur is a dragon hidden beneath the capital

=====================================
PRIESTS AND GRAVEWARDENS
=====================================

Priests handle:
- healing
- blessings
- weddings
- prayers
- ordinary funeral comfort

Gravewardens handle:
- body retrieval
- death blessings
- dangerous corpse-Qana sites
- battlefield cleanup
- curse management tied to death
- Lunacron risks

Rule:
- priests handle life-facing faith
- gravewardens handle death-facing faith

=====================================
KINGDOM-PAID BURIALS
=====================================

- Acerima pays for baseline burials.
- Nobles and wealthy families can buy additional prestige:
  - private tombs
  - mausoleums
  - extra rites
  - family crypts
  - luxury sun-shrines

=====================================
REGIONAL EXAMPLES
=====================================

Cask:
- wealthy desert retirement and burial village
- sun-focused death culture
- rich with nobles, merchants, guards, priestly offices, gravewarden stations, and funeral commerce

Anozira:
- vulnerable rural village
- tied to Ruzen mining and drought conditions
- represents the precarious survival side of Acerima rather than its luxury side

=====================================
DRAGON RUINS LAW
=====================================

- Claimed or monitored dragon ruins require permits.
- Newly discovered unclaimed ruins can be explored before the government arrives.
- Legal claim is possible if the explorer reports the discovery properly.
