Current locked rules:

- Heart of Fire is post-Dravrah, after Oren graduates and is assigned to Embrum.
- The Dravrah Incident threat was a skyscraper-scale Severence-created mutated Kraken, publicly misread as a natural Qana anomaly.
- Oren is monitored as a potential Calamity-level threat.
- Oren does not know Jötunn is real and interprets the voice as possible insanity.
- Dravrah leadership does not know Severence involvement, the Kraken's true nature, or Jötunn's existence.
- Qana replaces the older "mana/magic" framing. Qana is the universal quantum-energy substrate, and spells are shaped Qana outputs.
- Qana is also a biological requirement for pure field-dependent Qana creatures, which absorb ambient Qana directly instead of breathing oxygen.
- Humans are hybrid organisms: they breathe oxygen and replenish Qana indirectly through food, emotion, and mental processes.
- Qana recyclers regulate environmental Qana density, stabilize weather, and create Qana-depleted safe zones where Qana-dependent creatures cannot survive.
- Cities and villages are engineered safe zones; wild zones are higher-Qana environments with more unstable weather, creature activity, and Cron risk.
- A failed, overwhelmed, or sabotaged Qana recycler can allow creatures to return and can be weaponized by Severence.
- Qana creature threat tiers are documented human knowledge, not perfect natural truth; nations and guilds may know different things and may not share records.
- Qana creatures are mixed in temperament: many are hostile or dangerous on contact, but about forty percent are kind-natured, neutral, or potentially helpful unless betrayed or harmed.
- Qana creatures live wherever recycler coverage is absent or weak; open plains tend to have milder creatures, while deep forests, jungles, frontier mountains, severe-weather regions, and underground zones produce stronger adaptations.
- Qana creatures do not evolve by default; they adapt, with adaptation severity driven by environmental intensity.
- CAC studies and requests Qana creature materials but usually hires guilds, mercenaries, or knights rather than sending its own researchers to fight.
- Qana hunting includes combat, capture, harvesting, field documentation, environmental control, guild verification, and official knowledge updates.
- Qana hunts are hybrid and role-dependent: wizards specialize in spell output, swordsmen in physical fighting, kingdom knights are trained in both but limited by orders, and mercenaries form flexible mixed teams.
- Future Qana hunting gameplay should follow a loop of briefing, preparation, encounter, live player adaptation, outcome, and report.
- Briefing information can be incomplete, outdated, biased, or wrong; the player's field report can improve future Book of Qana Creatures records.
- Qana creatures resist their own element and are vulnerable to opposing elements; weaknesses are stable unless long-term adaptation changes them.
- The Book of Qana Creatures is shared kingdom knowledge updated through guild reports, verification missions, and the kingdom information department.
- Qana creatures can perform raw spell-like effects through emotional impulse, instinctual intent, and direct Qana-field manipulation, similar to Beastmen casting style.
- Older terms like mana/magic should be treated as deprecated, regional slang, religious language, or legacy terminology unless intentionally used.
- The combat/service ladder is regular knights, mercenaries, swordmasters, then Highguard. The regular public can rise to swordmaster, but Highguard requires selection or nomination by the king/royal family.
- Oren and Embrum found out about Anozira because the Chief of the Highguard assigned it to Embrum as an extra kingdom task.
- Highguard members often handle difficult local problems during free time because the kingdom pushes those issues onto them and because visible Highguard work reinforces their public celebrity.
- Embrum does not know Jötunn's identity or soul-binding details. He was told Oren is a Qana-unstable young man from Dravrah College whose output damaged the school while defeating a large creature.
- Embrum brought Oren to Anozira because Embrum is required to keep Oren under watch after Dravrah.
- Embrum interprets Oren's danger as unstable Qana output tied to anger or emotional pressure; if Oren loses control and threatens kingdom citizens, Embrum will take him down.
- Severence must not be mentioned in early playable scenes.
- The Anozira crisis should be framed as drought, dying srag, failing nairn/sinu, religious fear, and an unknown creature.
- The official drought creature name is Moglim: small rock-based creatures with Qana-heated shells, soft glowing cores, hidden legs, rolling/hopping movement, and living-meteor attack behavior.
- The Heart of Fire mystery chain is: Moglim are symptoms/scavengers, Ruzen mines are the practical heat-source clue site, the Pyroclast Titan is charging deep under Ruzen and evaporating underground reservoirs, and Severence is the hidden hand.
- A group/herd of Moglim is called a Mogul.
- The Ruzen mine descent should reveal the Titan as a tier 3 war golem with a Qana core. Embrum and Oren should not know Severence or the Embracron are involved.
- Later reveal: the fire Titan-class golem was deployed by Severence to contain and capture a forming Embracron in the Ruzen mines.
- Crons form only when extreme Qana density and elemental conditions converge around an exposed dead body; they are elemental force operating through corpse-shaped vessels, not conscious beings like dragons.
- After roughly one hour of Cron conversion, the original soul is gone and the body cannot be reclaimed as the person it once was.
- Lunacrons are the primary Cron exception: hostile on sight, soul-consuming, and able to form in dangerous battlefield conditions.
- The Anozira Embracron formed from an unrecovered miner's body in the unstable fire-weather zone deep in the Ruzen mines.
- The Ruzen mines should first read as a practical lead for extreme underground heat and failing wells, not an obvious boss dungeon.
- Early Severence hints should be deniable: geometric channels, unfamiliar runic scoring, foreign residue, missing high-density Ruzen clusters, and one recurring mark that is not identified yet.
- Anozira's nairn exports travel through the Port of Eldran to overseas Elven Kingdom buyers.
- Villager dialogue should reference economic pressure from nairn collapse, Brumel feed shortages, Eldran merchants/exporters, and belief in the royal family or Highest Order.
- Acerima's royal family descends from Jyra and Arthur; Jyana is the current queen, Aure is secretly captured by the Highest Order, and Arthur II operates in the field under the alias "Embrum."
- Female royal descendants tend toward direct Divine Qana authority and construct creation; male descendants tend toward healing and regenerative traits through Arthur's dragon lineage.
- House Ardwyn contributes healing, knowledge, law, and ecosystem tradition to the royal line; House Vey contributes strength, combat, and enforcement legacy.
- Acerima is a highly organized post-revolt human kingdom built on provinces, councils, ministries, military branches, and formalized survivor-line institutions.
- Human public culture emphasizes law, burial order, Highest Order devotion, provincial identity, Chainless tribal roots, and institutional pride.
- The Elven Kingdom is a female-led Shogunate with three major offices: Blade Court, Brush Court, and Moon-Seal Office.
- Use `trailblazers_elf_noble_families.txt` over older elf-politics notes when current Shogun-family names conflict.
- The current active Elven royal house is House Ikazuchi under Shogun Tomoe, with Hayate as her Wind Elf husband and Masako as a major royal trainer.
- Major human founding houses are Acerima, Vey, Ardwyn, Red-Quill, and Blue-Reed, with the Chainless as a respected non-noble ancestral people.
- Major active elf families now include House Ikazuchi, House Akagane, House Sugimori, House Yukikawa, and House Soryeong.
- House Soryeong contains the largest current hidden lore bomb: Mu-jin's missing wife was Oren's mother, and Oren is secretly tied to Dark Elf lineage and Severence experimentation.
- CAC regulates, approves, tests, and maintains Qana recyclers and new Qana technology; Highguard/local authorities handle enforcement.
- TAA controls teleportation towers and global travel as an independent, profit-driven network; trailblazers repair broken routes and checkpoints when TAA routes fail.
