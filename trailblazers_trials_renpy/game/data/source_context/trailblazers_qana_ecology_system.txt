TRAILBLAZERS — QANA ECOLOGY SYSTEM
Timestamp: April 23, 2026 (America/New_York)

PURPOSE:
This file defines how Qana functions as both an energy field and a biological requirement for different forms of life.
It also locks the ecological role of Qana recyclers, safe zones, wild zones, and Qana-dependent creatures.

=====================================
CORE RULE
=====================================

Qana is both:
- an energy field underlying matter, spell expression, and elemental behavior
- a biological requirement for some organisms

Not every organism depends on Qana the same way.

=====================================
TWO TYPES OF LIFE
=====================================

1. HUMANS — HYBRID SYSTEM

Humans rely primarily on oxygen respiration.
They breathe through lungs and are not harmed by low-Qana environments.

Humans absorb and replenish Qana indirectly through:
- food, digestion, rest, and bodily health feeding the stomach pool
- emotion, memory, motivation, grief, love, fear, and resolve feeding the heart pool
- meditation, focus, stillness, and mental structuring feeding the head pool

Humans are dual-dependent organisms:
- oxygen for life support
- Qana for spell expression, Qana reserves, and advanced physical/spiritual function

2. QANA CREATURES — PURE FIELD-DEPENDENT LIFE

Qana creatures rely on ambient Qana rather than oxygen.
They do not need lungs and do not require traditional organ layouts.

They absorb Qana directly through:
- skin
- surface membranes
- body field
- non-traditional biological structures

This allows extreme variation in body shape, organ structure, and life cycle.

=====================================
QANA BREATHING MECHANIC
=====================================

Qana creatures "breathe" by exchanging with the surrounding Qana field:

1. Ambient Qana enters through the body's surface or field boundary.
2. Internal structures convert the absorbed Qana state.
3. The resolved Qana becomes usable biological energy.

This replaces oxygen exchange.

Qana creatures do not inhale air to survive.
They convert surrounding Qana into an oxygen-equivalent life process.

=====================================
QANA RECYCLERS
=====================================

Qana recyclers regulate environmental Qana density.
They are ecosystem regulators, not only weather stabilizers.

Full function:

1. Reduce Qana saturation
- prevents dangerous Qana buildup
- lowers ambient Qana density

2. Stabilize the environment
- keeps weather consistent
- prevents elemental buildup
- reduces field instability

3. Deny Qana to Qana-dependent creatures
- removes what those creatures "breathe"
- makes the area uninhabitable for pure field-dependent life

Result:
Qana recyclers create Qana-depleted safe zones where humans can live with lower monster risk.

=====================================
SAFE ZONES AND WILD ZONES
=====================================

SAFE ZONES — HUMAN TERRITORY

Safe zones are artificially stabilized areas.
They usually have:
- low ambient Qana density
- stable weather
- reduced elemental buildup
- working Qana recyclers
- few or no Qana-dependent creatures

Cities and villages are not naturally safe.
They are engineered safe zones maintained by Qana technology and civic infrastructure.

WILD ZONES — DANGER AREAS

Wild zones usually have:
- high ambient Qana density
- unstable weather
- creature-heavy ecosystems
- higher Cron risk
- more frequent elemental anomalies

Qana creatures are not always "invading" human territory.
In many cases, they are returning to environments that became habitable to them again after recycler failure or sabotage.

=====================================
QANA CREATURE TIERS AND DOCUMENTATION
=====================================

Creature strength is documented, not automatically known.
Threat tiers are assigned after someone observes, survives, studies, captures, or defeats the creature.

This creates a Monster Hunter-style knowledge model:
- a creature's real strength may be unknown before documentation
- different nations may hold different records
- some nations, guilds, schools, and research bodies know more than others
- not all creature information is shared openly between nations
- threat classification can be wrong, outdated, or region-specific

Use tier language when relevant, but treat it as a human knowledge system rather than a perfect natural law.

Known broad threat language:
- Hiccup Tier: low threat
- Misfortune Tier: mid threat
- Disaster Tier: high threat
- Calamity Tier: extreme threat

=====================================
QANA CREATURE TEMPERAMENT
=====================================

Qana creatures are not all hostile.

Current broad rule:
- many are aggressive or dangerous on contact
- about forty percent are kind-natured, neutral, or potentially helpful on first contact
- even kind creatures can turn hostile if betrayed, wounded, trapped, or attacked
- temperament can shift with Qana density, injury, fear, starvation, territorial pressure, or environmental stress

Example kind-natured creature:

AETHRUM
- A Qana creature that can consciously aid in sustaining a person's life.
- Its help is intentional, not accidental.
- It should be treated as proof that Qana creatures can be benevolent, empathetic, or cooperative by nature.

Do not write Qana creatures as automatically evil.
Most dangerous encounters come from ecology, territory, hunger, fear, Qana stress, or human betrayal.

=====================================
QANA CREATURE HABITATS
=====================================

Qana creatures can live anywhere recycler coverage is absent, weak, failed, or overwhelmed.

Threat level depends heavily on biome:

OPEN PLAINS AND LOW-SEVERITY BIOMES
- Qana is more dispersed.
- Creatures tend to be lower threat and less aggressive.
- Examples include slimes and Moglim.
- These areas are commonly used for student training when conditions are controlled enough.

HARSH HIGH-QANA BIOMES
- Dense forests
- Deep jungles
- High frontier mountains
- Severe-weather regions
- Deep underground zones

In these places, Qana creatures have the natural advantage.
They tend to become stronger, more territorial, and more aggressive because the environment supports stronger field-dependent life.

=====================================
ADAPTATION
=====================================

Qana creatures do not evolve in the normal long-term species-change sense.
They adapt.

Adaptations are shaped by environmental pressure.
The severity of adaptation depends on how intense the environment is and how strongly it forces the creature to change.

Important distinction:
- Evolution is not the default creature rule.
- Adaptation is the default creature rule.
- High-Qana or extreme-biome pressure can produce severe adaptations.

=====================================
CAC INVOLVEMENT WITH CREATURES
=====================================

CAC are inventors, scientists, regulators, and researchers.
They do not normally go out to defeat Qana creatures themselves.

CAC often need:
- creature body parts
- rare materials
- biological samples
- field data
- Qana-reactive tissue
- hazard reports

How CAC obtains these:
- job forms sent to guilds
- contracts sent to mercenaries
- requests sent to the capital
- knight deployments when the crown accepts the request
- purchased materials from licensed hunters, adventurers, or field teams

CAC usually avoids risking its own researchers unless the mission requires direct scientific observation.

=====================================
IMPORTANT CONSEQUENCES
=====================================

1. Villages depend on recyclers.

If a recycler fails or is overwhelmed:
- Qana spikes
- weather destabilizes
- Qana creatures can enter or survive nearby
- Cron risk increases in exposed-death conditions

2. Removing or disabling a recycler can be weaponized.

Saboteurs can:
- flood an area with Qana
- force creature movement
- create Cron-prone zones
- destabilize weather
- make human safe zones collapse

3. Cities are engineered ecosystems.

Urban safety depends on:
- recycler infrastructure
- monitoring
- maintenance
- controlled Qana density

4. Qana creatures follow ecology, not morality.

They are often drawn by restored or increased ambient Qana rather than evil intent.

=====================================
ANOZIRA IMPLICATIONS
=====================================

The Anozira arc should use this logic:

- The Qana recycler normally keeps Anozira stable enough for human settlement and farming.
- The Pyroclast Titan's charging deep in Ruzen overwhelms the regional balance.
- Underground reservoirs heat and evaporate, causing drought.
- Environmental Qana instability and heat residue make the area more habitable to Moglim.
- Moglim appear because conditions now support them, not because they are masterminds.
- The visible village problem is drought and creature appearance.
- The hidden structural problem is that the engineered safe zone is failing.

=====================================
SEVERENCE IMPLICATIONS
=====================================

Severence can exploit recycler logic by:

- disabling Qana recyclers
- overwhelming stable zones with Qana
- manipulating creature movement
- creating Cron-prone environments
- hiding attacks behind "natural" Qana instability

This lets Severence weaponize ecology without always appearing directly.

=====================================
CLEAN LORE VERSION
=====================================

Qana creatures are biological entities that rely on ambient Qana rather than oxygen.
Instead of breathing through lungs, they absorb Qana directly through their bodies and convert it into usable energy.
This allows extreme variation in form, including non-traditional biological structures.

Qana recyclers regulate environmental Qana density.
By reducing ambient Qana levels, they stabilize weather and prevent dangerous Qana buildup.
This also makes the environment uninhabitable for Qana-dependent creatures, effectively creating safe zones for human settlement.

Humans are not affected by low-Qana environments because they rely primarily on oxygen for respiration and obtain Qana indirectly through food, emotion, and mental processes.
