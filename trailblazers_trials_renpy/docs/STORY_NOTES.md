# Story Notes

## Oren Timeline Anchor

- Oren is the human protagonist.
- Heart of Fire takes place after the Dravrah incident and after Oren graduates from Dravrah.
- Oren is assigned to Master Embrum as a squire under Highguard supervision.
- Embrum was assigned by the kingdom to watch Oren after Dravrah and was told Oren is a Qana-unstable young man whose output destroyed a large creature while also damaging parts of the school.
- Embrum interprets Oren's danger as uncontrolled Qana output, especially under anger or pressure. If Oren loses control and threatens kingdom citizens, Embrum believes it is his duty to take Oren down.
- Oren and Embrum came to Anozira because the Chief of the Highguard assigned Embrum to investigate it, and Embrum brought Oren because he is required to keep Oren under watch.
- Authorities consider Oren unstable, dangerous, and a potential Calamity-level threat, but he is not executed because he saved Dravrah.
- The Dravrah Incident involved a skyscraper-scale mutated Kraken publicly believed to be a natural Qana anomaly.
- The Kraken was actually created by Severence to eliminate noble bloodline students, but this is hidden from the public and Dravrah leadership.
- Oren is depressed, isolated, and distrustful of himself during the Anozira Village arc.

## Oren And Jötunn

- Jötunn is the dragon soul forced into Oren's heart by Severence.
- Jötunn is not another version of Oren, but Oren does not know that.
- Oren believes the voice may be his own mind breaking.
- Jötunn claims he is a dragon, that he was sealed, and that he will regain his body.
- Oren rejects this because dragons have been sealed for 2226 years and there is no living proof.
- Code uses `jotunn` without the accent for safer Ren'Py scripting.
- Current early-game UI labels Jötunn as `Voice` to preserve Oren's uncertainty.
- Pronunciation note: "yotun."

## Hidden Knowledge Rules

- Do not mention Severence in early playable scenes.
- Oren does not know Severence exists, that Jötunn is real, that his body contains two souls, or the origin of his power.
- Dravrah leadership does not know Severence involvement, the Kraken's true nature, or Jötunn's existence.
- Embrum does not know Jötunn's true identity or the soul-binding details.
- Embrum does not understand Oren's situation as an inner entity. He frames it as unstable Qana control.
- The unknown figure who knows Oren's condition must not be referenced or revealed yet.
- Anozira villagers should frame the crisis as drought, divine testing, natural imbalance, or an unknown creature.

## Qana System

- Qana replaces the older mana/magic framing.
- Qana is the universal quantum-energy field/substrate underlying matter, energy, biology, thought-linked spell expression, and elemental behavior.
- Spells are shaped outputs, programmed expressions, or local collapses of Qana.
- Qana quanta are the particle-like packets or excitations of the Qana field.
- The Qana field fills reality; Qana quanta appear where the field is excited, bound, shaped, or collapsed.
- Wave-like Qana is diffuse, ambient, and possibility-rich. Particle-like Qana is localized, measurable, and physically interactive.
- Qana cannot be created or destroyed; it changes state, pattern, density, and expression.
- Qana prefers stable patterns and falls into the strongest nearby influence: heat, pressure, water, emotion, language, runes, tool geometry, light, death, or environmental conditions.
- Elements are stable attractor states of Qana, not separate fantasy forces.
- Fire is heat-dominant Qana collapse; water is cohesion/flow-dominant collapse; earth is pressure/binding-dominant collapse; lightning is discharge-dominant collapse; flora is growth-pattern collapse.
- Older words like mana and magic are deprecated, regional slang, religious language, or legacy terminology unless intentionally used.

## Qana Ecology

- Qana can be both an energy field and a biological requirement depending on the organism.
- Humans are hybrid organisms: they breathe oxygen through lungs and replenish Qana indirectly through food, emotion, meditation, and mental processing.
- Qana creatures are pure field-dependent lifeforms that do not rely on oxygen and do not require traditional organ layouts.
- Qana creatures absorb ambient Qana directly through skin, surface membranes, body fields, or other biological structures, then resolve it into usable energy.
- Qana recyclers regulate environmental Qana density, stabilize weather, prevent elemental buildup, and deny Qana to field-dependent creatures.
- Working recyclers create Qana-depleted safe zones for human settlement.
- Cities and villages are engineered safe zones, not naturally safe ecosystems.
- Wild zones are high-Qana areas with unstable weather, creature-heavy ecosystems, and higher Cron risk.
- If a recycler fails or is overwhelmed, Qana creatures can return because the area becomes habitable to them again.
- Disabling or overwhelming a recycler can be weaponized by Severence to flood an area with Qana, move creatures, destabilize weather, or create Cron-prone conditions.
- Qana creature threat tiers are assigned after documentation by survivors, hunters, guilds, nations, schools, CAC researchers, or field teams.
- Different nations may know different things about the same creature, and those records are not always shared.
- Many Qana creatures are dangerous or hostile on contact, but roughly forty percent are kind-natured, neutral, or potentially helpful on first contact.
- Aethrums are a kind-natured Qana creature that can consciously aid in sustaining a person's life.
- Even kind Qana creatures can turn hostile if betrayed, harmed, trapped, or threatened.
- Open plains and low-severity biomes have more dispersed Qana and generally milder creatures such as slimes and Moglim; school students can train in controlled plains biomes.
- Harsh high-Qana biomes such as deep jungles, deep forests, high frontier mountains, severe-weather regions, and underground zones produce stronger and more aggressive creature adaptations.
- Qana creatures do not evolve by default; they adapt, and adaptation severity depends on environmental pressure.

## Qana Hunting

- Qana hunting is the structured human interaction with Qana creatures: combat, capture, harvesting, documentation, and environmental control.
- Qana hunts are hybrid and role-dependent.
- Qana casters or wizards have strong spell output but weak physical combat and usually rely on distance, control, and elemental pressure.
- Swordsmen or non-Qana-trained fighters are strong physical fighters but may only know basic spells like tiny ember creation or push pulse.
- Kingdom knights receive mandatory sword and Qana training, making them versatile and disciplined, but they are limited by kingdom orders and cannot freely take every job.
- Mercenaries have mixed skill sets and can take many jobs for pay if their guild master allows it; serious hunts often need teams with multiple specialties.
- Future Qana hunting gameplay should follow this loop: briefing, preparation, encounter, live player adaptation, outcome, and report.
- Briefings may come from teachers, guild reps, knight officers, CAC contacts, or local authorities and can include known creature type, weaknesses, habitat, threat tier, and objective.
- Briefing information can be incomplete, outdated, biased, or wrong, especially when creature records differ by region or adaptation has changed behavior.
- The encounter phase reveals whether the creature matches expectations, including behavior, temperament, raw Qana effects, biome strength, or new adaptation evidence.
- The live adaptation phase is the main player challenge: react to environment, creature behavior, team weaknesses, elemental mismatch, failed preparation, and unreliable intel.
- Reports after hunts can update the Book of Qana Creatures, reputation, school grades, CAC research, kingdom records, and future briefing accuracy.
- Qana creatures resist their own element and are vulnerable to opposing elements.
- Creature weaknesses are stable in the short term; adaptation takes about 28 days to 6 months and cannot happen mid-fight.
- Hunt outcomes include killing, capturing, harvesting, driving away, relocating, documenting, withdrawing, or stabilizing the environment.
- Almost no creature body goes unused because materials are valuable to poor communities, guilds, CAC researchers, smiths, healers, Qana technicians, and spell-tool makers.
- The Book of Qana Creatures is shared kingdom knowledge used by schools, guilds, knights, CAC researchers, and local authorities.
- New creature information must be reported to a guild, verified by a guild mission, sent to the kingdom information department, and accepted before it becomes official.
- The kingdom ignores unverified creature information or treats it as rumor.
- Qana creatures can naturally perform raw spell-like effects through emotional impulse, instinctual intent, and direct Qana-field manipulation, similar to Beastmen casting style.

## Highguard

- The kingdom combat/service ladder is regular knights, mercenaries, swordmasters, then Highguard.
- People from the regular public can rise up to swordmaster.
- Highguard members must be selected or nominated by the king or royal family.
- Highguard members are among the most capable people in the kingdom and are treated as superstrong anomalies and celebrities.
- Because Highguard are so capable, the kingdom often gives them difficult extra tasks when they are not protecting the world from major threats.
- These tasks are often local problems the kingdom is too lazy, slow, or politically unwilling to solve directly.
- Highguard members handling everyday-but-difficult problems keeps them visible around the kingdom and reinforces their celebrity status.
- The Anozira crisis reached Embrum through the Chief of the Highguard, not because Embrum randomly discovered it.
- Embrum views Anozira as both an assigned investigation and a controlled field test for Oren under supervision.

## Acerima Royal Family

- Acerima's founding royal figures are Jyra, daughter of the Highest Order, and Arthur, the Dragon of Healing Light.
- Jyra is the source of Divine Qana authority in the bloodline and rarely appears except in guidance-heavy or emotionally critical moments.
- Arthur is alive and hidden beneath the capital; his lineage is the source of royal healing and regenerative traits.
- Their children are Aure and Jyana.
- Jyana is the current Queen of Acerima and rules with council support.
- Aure is officially missing to the public but is secretly captured by the Highest Order and studied for Divine Qana and healing traits.
- Jyana married Luther Vey, a swordmaster tied to House Vey.
- Aure married Selena Ardwyn, whose line contributes healing, knowledge, and ecosystem tradition.
- The royal grandchildren are Jysca, Arthur II, Rin, and Ruther.
- Jysca is politically driven and pressured toward future queenship.
- Rin and Ruther train in the Elven Kingdom.
- Arthur II is Embrum: he travels under an alias, serves in the Highguard, searches for Aure, hides divine healing abilities, and uses fire Qana in combat instead.
- Female royal descendants tend toward direct Divine Qana authority and construct creation.
- Male royal descendants tend toward healing and regeneration through Arthur's dragon lineage.
- House Vey represents strength, combat, labor, guild leadership, and military legacy.
- House Ardwyn represents healing, knowledge, law, medicine, potion work, and information roles.

## Acerima Politics And Culture

- Acerima is a post-Dragon-Revolt human kingdom that defines itself through law, order, institutional structure, and survival turned into government.
- Public human identity combines Highest Order devotion, Chainless roots, provincial pride, burial law, academy culture, and fear of uncontrolled Qana.
- Queen Jyana rules through a council and multiple branches rather than handling every kingdom matter directly.
- Acerima is divided into provinces with a city, villages, forts, and a noble governor.
- The military is governed by the Aceriman War Table rather than one supreme general.
- Major branches are Crown Army, Tide Fleet, Sky Corps, Province Guard, and Highguard.
- Knighthood is legally open, but nobles have strong structural advantages in training and access.
- Priests handle life-facing faith; gravewardens handle death-facing faith, corpse danger, and Lunacron risk.
- Cask represents Acerima's wealthy burial and sun-faith culture; Anozira represents its harsh survival side.

## Human Noble Families

- House Acerima is the royal line and the mythic core of the kingdom itself.
- House Vey is the great military/strength house and anchors Crown Army-style service.
- House Ardwyn is the healing, medicine, priesthood, gravewarden, and legal-preservation house.
- House Red-Quill anchors archives, intelligence, spell records, and information bureaucracy.
- House Blue-Reed anchors water transport, naval power, trade routes, ports, diving, and salvage.
- The Chainless are not a noble house; they are an old ancestral people preserving pre-kingdom human roots.
- Human noble logic is registry-driven: surname, line prestige, and House Registry status all matter.

## Elven Politics And Culture

- The Elven Kingdom is a female-led Shogunate with house-first identity, formal hierarchy, Latin sacred spell-poetry, and Mother Qana worship.
- The three great offices are Blade Court, Brush Court, and Moon-Seal Office.
- Female elves are the primary warrior class; male elves are expected to become shamans under the Mother Qana contract system.
- Dark Elves oversee death handling, soul magic, corpse safety, and Lunacron prevention through the Moon-Seal Office.
- Wind Elves are rare global nomads tied to the Time Dragon and are not a normal territorial house.
- The Black River Crisis is a major historical reason Dark Elf authority became legally necessary.

## Anozira Village

- Anozira is an agricultural village in Acerima.
- Its primary export is high-quality nairn used as livestock feed.
- Anozira nairn is valued because it supports faster livestock growth and heavier Brumel mass gain.
- Trade flows from Anozira to the Port of Eldran, then overseas to the Elven Kingdom.
- The Elven Kingdom depends on imported feed because redwood environments limit natural agriculture.
- The drought threatens village survival, Eldran trade profits, and overseas livestock supply.
- Villager dialogue should mix religious fear with economic anxiety: empty nairn carts, Eldran merchants, Brumel feed shortages, and hope that the royal family or Highest Order will intervene.

## CAC, TAA, And Moglim

- CAC means Central Arcane Commission. It regulates, approves, tests, and maintains new Qana technology such as Qana recyclers.
- Qana recyclers are ecosystem regulators: they reduce ambient Qana saturation, stabilize weather, and make areas safer for humans by making them harder for Qana-dependent creatures to inhabit.
- CAC studies Qana creatures and often needs body parts, rare materials, samples, Qana-reactive tissue, and field data, but usually sends job forms to guilds, mercenary contracts, or requests to the capital/knights instead of risking its own researchers in combat.
- CAC does not control traditional spellcasting; enforcement is handled by Highguard and local authorities.
- TAA means Teleportation Authority Association. It controls teleportation towers and the global travel network as an independent, profit-driven organization.
- TAA access requires payment and a TAA-issued ID. Trailblazers repair broken routes and checkpoints when TAA routes fail.
- The official drought creature is the Moglim: a small rock-based creature about yoga-ball sized, with a cracked stone shell and soft glowing organic core.
- Moglim roll and hop on hidden legs, heat their rocky exterior with internal Qana, and launch themselves like living meteors.
- Moglim are low intelligence and low threat individually, but dangerous in groups and often appear near larger Qana anomalies.
- A group or herd of Moglim is called a Mogul.

## Heart Of Fire Escalation

- The Moglim fight is a symptom, not the real crisis.
- Moglim appear near Anozira because the engineered safe zone is failing and conditions are becoming more habitable to Qana-dependent creatures.
- Do not rush directly from the Moglim fight into the mines. Use an evening Anozira interlude to show grief, medical consequences, warm wells, and villagers trying to make sense of the crisis.
- The tavern scene can reveal that a grieving drunk father lost his miner son in Ruzen and never got a body back. This should foreshadow the later Embracron truth without naming Crons or Severence.
- The medic-cleric office should frame the crisis through burns, drought fever, and civic fear, not lore exposition.
- Ruzen mines are Anozira's second-most valuable export after nairn and are the next practical lead.
- The mines are shut down because of recent heat bursts, overheated tools, steam pressure, glowing Ruzen seams, and temperatures rising too fast to work safely.
- The Ruzen miners were used to normal fire-Qana weather, but the mine changed after a deep expedition for larger Ruzen crystals two to three months ago.
- The foreman pushed that expedition to meet quota before the next Eldran shipment and make extra money.
- Two miners died in the deep flames during a Qana buildup and flame-tornado event; one had a father waiting, and one had a mother and little sister waiting.
- The village reads the drought partly as punishment for those deaths.
- The drought is not caused by fire-Qana leaking into the fields. Severence placed a Titan-class fire golem deep within Ruzen, where it charges on Ruzen fire energy and lava heat.
- The Titan's charging superheats the mountain and ground, evaporating the underground water reservoirs connected to Anozira's wells.
- Wells dry out or release steam because the reservoirs below them are being heated, not because the wells are cursed or supernaturally drained.
- Crops fail because the soil is too hot for young srag, nairn, and sinu to survive in their normal environmental temperature range.
- Embrum should initially conclude Ruzen is connected to a deep heat source that is cooking the reservoirs under Anozira, not that fire-Qana is venting into the fields.
- Hidden Severence hints should remain deniable: geometric cuts, unfamiliar runic scoring, strange residue, missing high-density Ruzen clusters, and one unidentified mark.
- Oren should feel something below the mountain is consuming power because Jötunn recognizes the shaped heat, but Oren should distrust that insight.
- The Titan reveal should be environmental and huge: a hill or horizon line moves, glowing cracks appear, stone breaks loose, and Embrum realizes it is not a mountain.
- In the mine sequence, Embrum identifies the Titan as a tier 3 golem: an illegal weapon of war unless summoned by kingdom order.
- The Titan has a Qana core, a crystal heart/battery powering the golem.
- Oren and Embrum defeat it by having Embrum distract and contain its movements while Oren charges a spear strike into the Qana core.
- Oren's strike combines his own fire-Qana output with Pure Qana pressure from Jötunn, though Oren still interprets this through confusion rather than confirmed dragon truth.
- Later reveal: Severence deployed the fire Titan-class golem to contain and capture a forming Embracron in Ruzen.
- The Embracron formed from an unrecovered miner's body in the unstable fire-weather convergence zone deep in the mines.
- The Titan remains the visible central threat for the early arc, but the Embracron explains why Severence was operating there at all.

## Crons

- Crons are ancient elemental creatures formed when extreme Qana density and elemental environmental conditions converge around an exposed dead body.
- All Crons are corpse-born with no exceptions.
- Dragons are conscious biological beings stabilized around Pure Qana; Crons are elemental force operating through corpse-shaped vessels.
- Crons are not truly intelligent or fully conscious. They behave according to the dominant element that created them.
- If a body is left unburied in a highly unstable elemental zone, surrounding Qana can overtake it, decompose it into the dominant element, and reanimate it as a Cron.
- After roughly one hour of exposure/conversion, the original soul is gone and the body cannot be reclaimed as the person it once was.
- Legendary Crons are not formed differently; they are more dangerous because they are born from more powerful corpses, such as dragon husks.
- Most Crons are unintelligent, instinct-driven, dormant unless disturbed, and drawn toward concentrated Qana.
- Known Cron types include Lunacron, Solacron, Embracron, Aquacron, Electricron, and Floracron.
- Lunacrons are the primary exception: hostile on sight, soul-consuming, and often born from uncleansed battlefields under lunar energy.
- Burial and corpse recovery are taken seriously across the world because unrecovered bodies can become dangerous in unstable Qana zones.

## Current Writing Direction

The opening should make the voice feel like a frightening internal pressure, not confirmed lore. Jötunn can help Oren survive, but every time Oren leans on him, the player should feel the danger of giving the hidden dragon more influence.
